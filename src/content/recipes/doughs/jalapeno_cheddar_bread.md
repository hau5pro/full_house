## Ingredients

**Dry Mix**

- 3½ cups (420g) _bread flour_
- 1½ tsp _kosher salt_
- 2 cups _cheddar cheese_, shredded
- 1 _jalapeño_, diced

**Wet Mix**

- 2 cups warm _water_
- 2¼ tsp _instant yeast_

**Topping**

- ⅓–½ cup _cheddar cheese_, shredded
- 10 _jalapeño_ slices

## Directions

1. Mix flour, salt, cheese, and jalapeño in a medium bowl.
2. Combine warm water and yeast in a large bowl. Add flour mixture and stir with a rubber spatula until no dry streaks remain.
3. Using a spatula, fold dough from edges toward center 8–10 times. Cover and rise 1 hour in a warm spot.
4. Repeat spatula folding. Cover and rise another hour.
5. Halfway through second rise, place a covered dutch oven in the oven and preheat to 450°F for 30 minutes.
6. Flour a work surface. Turn dough out, flip, and brush off excess flour.
7. Fold corners toward center 6–8 times to form a rough ball. Transfer to parchment paper.
8. Score the top, spray lightly with water, and top with reserved cheese and jalapeño slices.
9. Lower dough and parchment into hot dutch oven. Cover and bake 30 minutes.
10. Remove lid and bake 20 more minutes until deep golden.
11. Cool on a wire rack 1–2 hours before slicing.

#### No dutch oven?

1. Boil about 1½ cups of water. Pour into a metal or ceramic pan on the bottom oven rack.
2. Place shaped dough on parchment on a baking sheet on the middle rack.
3. Bake at 450°F for 45–50 minutes until deep golden.

## Notes

- **Dough texture:** This is a high-hydration dough — it will be loose and extremely sticky, not shapeable like traditional dough. Use a rubber spatula for folding.
- **Flour:** All-purpose flour works but may yield a denser loaf.
- **Jalapeños:** Canned jalapeños are fine as a substitute.
- **Dutch oven:** Use at least a 3.5-quart dutch oven.
